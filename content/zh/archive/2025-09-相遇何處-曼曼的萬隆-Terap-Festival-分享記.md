打開−當代與 P.M.S. 的成員曼曼在八月受邀前往印尼萬隆，參與第二屆 Terap Festival——一個在公共空間舉行的劇場藝術節，透過表演藝術探索、解讀並重新想像城市空間。

今年的 Terap Festival 以 「我們街上的城市：行動的權利與城市的權利」 (Kota di Jalan Kita: Hak Mobilitas & Hak atas Kota) 為主題，邀請 Ferial Afiff 和 Trianzani Sulshi 擔任策展人，並以策展概念 「Caang Mumbul Dina Batok」 為核心。這句標語改編自巽他語諺語，字面意思是「殼中明亮飛升」（多指椰子殼），象徵城市越來越現代、光鮮亮麗，為發展自豪，但同時提醒我們，生活在城市成長「泡泡」中的人們，可能正面臨困境或被邊緣化。
本屆藝術節的參與方式除了受邀藝術家，也包括公開徵件，並邀請上一屆的藝術家回歸參與。在這場分享中，曼曼將帶來她參與藝術節的第一人稱視角，分享如何在有限的拜訪時間裡回應策展主題，以及她的創作如何在跨文化脈絡下逐步成形。她也將介紹其他受邀藝術家與當地社群合作的作品，以及相關的創作反思。

講座活動｜2025.09.21（日）15:00 - 17:00

 “Where do we meet?” — manman at the Terap Festival, Bandung

In August, OCAC (Open Contemporary Art Center) and P.M.S. member manman was invited to Bandung, Indonesia, to participate in the second edition of the Terap Festival—a public-space theatre festival dedicated to exploring, interpreting, and transforming the city through performative art.
This year’s festival, themed “Cities on Our Street: The Right to Mobility & the Right to the City” (Kota di Jalan Kita: Hak Mobilitas & Hak atas Kota), invited Ferial Afiff and Trianzani Sulshi as curators, presenting the curatorial concept “Caang Mumbul Dina Batok”, adapted from a Sundanese proverb. Literally, it means “brightly shining, soaring in the shell,” mostly referring to a coconut shell. The concept symbolizes a city becoming increasingly modern, shiny, and proud of its development, while also cautioning that people living inside the “bubble” of urban growth may be suffering or marginalized amidst this progress.

The festival’s format not only invited artists to participate, but also included open calls and extended invitations to two artists from the previous edition. In this sharing, manman will offer a first-hand perspective on her experience: how she engaged with the curatorial theme within a limited timeframe, how her work gradually took shape in a cross-cultural context, and her relevant reflections. She will also highlight works by fellow participating artists, many of whom collaborated closely with local communities in Bandung.
